## 3. OUR MISSION — REWRITTEN

### New Copy (Single Paragraph, Founder-Forward)

```
Here's the thing: when I started SnusFriend, I was tired of massive
generic shops that treated customers like transaction numbers. I wanted
to build something smaller, more thoughtful—a place where you could find
exactly what you wanted without wading through bloat.

Now, we stock 2,200+ pouches from 50+ brands. Every single one hand-picked.
We curate, we don't just list. New flavours drop every week. We've written
43 in-depth guides so you can pick the right strength and flavour without
guessing. And we've built a rewards programme that actually rewards loyalty—
spin wheel, avatars, quests, a leaderboard—because buying pouches should
be fun, not a chore.

Our mission is simple: be the most trusted, most fun nicotine pouch shop
in Europe. Honest prices. Fast dispatch. Real selection. A real person
answers your emails.
```

**Why it works:**
- Opens with founder voice (personal, direct)
- Explains the "why" before the "what"
- Weaves in differentiators naturally (curation, guides, gamification, community)
- Closes with a bold, credible claim backed by specifics
- No corporate jargon

**Tone cues:** Casual ("Here's the thing"), self-aware ("I was tired of"), direct ("hand-picked", "real person")

## 4. WHO WE ARE — REWRITTEN (2–3 Paragraphs)

### Paragraph 1 (The Founder)

```
I'm Daniel, and I run SnusFriend. We're based in the EU, we work directly
with manufacturers and authorised distributors, and we only stock nicotine
pouches—full stop. No tobacco, no marketing fluff. Just the good stuff.

I started this because I wanted a shop I'd actually want to use.
A place where the selection wasn't bloated, where prices were fair,
and where asking a question got you a real answer from a real person,
not a bot. That's still the rule here.
```

### Paragraph 2 (The Team Ethos)

```
We're small. That's intentional. It means every customer email gets
genuine attention—no tier-2 support walls, no canned responses. I handle
a lot of the product curation and customer stuff myself. My team knows
every brand we stock and why it's here.

You'll notice our product pages have honest reviews, our guides dig into
flavour profiles and nicotine levels properly, and our loyalty programme
is built around actual fun (spin wheel, avatars, leaderboard) because
we think buying pouches shouldn't be boring.
```

### Paragraph 3 (The Differentiator)

```
We're not trying to be Haypp or Northerner. Those shops are big, clean,
corporate—and they do that well. But they're generic. We're curated.
We're opinionated. We talk to our community about what's worth stocking
and what's not. And we've only been doing this for a few years, but
we've already built something people actually enjoy using.
```

**Why these work:**
- Paragraph 1: Founder story without being self-aggrandising; establishes credibility
- Paragraph 2: Shows small-team advantage as a feature, not an apology; lists concrete differentiators
- Paragraph 3: Acknowledges competitors respectfully, then positions ourselves as the alternative; doesn't sound defensive
- All three use contractions, casual rhythm, and specifics rather than platitudes

## 11. IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Copy Structure
- **Hero:** Use Option 1 for tagline, Option 1 for subheading (both are strong, paired well)
- **Mission:** Replace current 2-sentence version with the full paragraph—it reads better and shows founder voice
- **Who We Are:** Replace current 2-paragraph section with the new 3-paragraph version
- **Value Cards:** Swap titles and copy as shown; keep the 2-column grid layout
- **Comparison Section:** Add this *after* the values cards, *before* "Responsible Use" section (new section)
- **CTAs:** Keep current structure; consider upgrading secondary CTA text to "Learn Your Strength"

### Design Considerations
- **Hero image:** If using a product flat-lay, shoot at 45° angle with natural light; avoid white background (feels sterile)
- **Icons:** Keep consistent line-weight and colour palette; test at small sizes (24px, 32px) to ensure readability
- **"Who We Are" visual:** If it's a photo of Daniel, make sure it's genuine and approachable (no corporate headshots)
- **Typography:** Keep Space Grotesk for H1 (tagline); use Inter for body copy. All copy here is written for a sans-serif, readable font

### Voice Checklist
- ✅ No corporate jargon ("ecosystem", "synergy", "best-in-class", "revolutionary")
- ✅ Short sentences (mostly 10–20 words)
- ✅ Contractions used freely ("I'm", "we're", "don't", "it's")
- ✅ Specific, not vague ("2,200+", "43 guides", "2–5 days", "hand-picked")
- ✅ Founder voice present (first-person in Mission and Who We Are)
- ✅ Competitive positioning is respectful, not defensive
- ✅ British English maintained (no "center", "color", etc.)
