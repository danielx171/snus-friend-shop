UX Improvements

High-Impact Fixes from Site Audit

Found by reviewing homepage, product cards, PDP, brands page, header, and footer. Ranked by user impact, not complexity.

🎯

Strength Colour Strip on Product Cards 30 min

Every product card across the entire site

A 3px colour band at the bottom of each product image. Green = light, blue = normal, orange = strong, red = extra strong, purple = super strong. No label needed — users learn the system in one browse. This mirrors how real tin lids use colour to signal strength, and it makes scanning a grid of 20+ products instant instead of reading each card's text.

Unified 5-Dot Strength Scale 20 min

Product cards + PDP — replace current single coloured dot + text label

Right now cards show a single coloured dot with "Normal" or "Strong" text, but the PDP shows a 5-dot scale. This inconsistency means users learn the system on the detail page, then can't find it on the card. Unify to 5 dots everywhere — filled dots = strength level, colour matches the strength tier. Scannable at a glance without reading.

💰

Show "1 can" Next to Price on Cards 15 min

Product cards — price area

Cards show "€3.08" but don't say what that gets you. Is that per pouch? Per can? Per 10-pack? Users have to click through to find out. Adding "1 can" in tiny muted text next to the price eliminates a friction point before add-to-cart. The PDP already calculates multi-pack savings — this primes users for the upsell.

Capitalise Flavour Labels on Cards 10 min

Product cards — flavour badge

Cards show raw data keys like "fruit", "mint", "berry" but the PDP shows proper labels "Fruit", "Mint", "Berry". Small inconsistency, but it makes the cards look unfinished. Apply the same flavorLabels map that the PDP uses.

Brand Page Header — Tin Lid Ring Pattern 20 min

Individual brand pages (/brands/zyn, /brands/velo, etc.)

The brand page header already uses a gradient with the brand colour. Add faint concentric ring lines (like the grip rings on a real tin lid viewed from above) as a CSS-only background detail. No new assets needed — just 3 positioned circles with ~4% white opacity. Grounds the header in the product without being flashy.

Add-to-Cart "Snap" Micro-Animation 15 min

All Add to Cart buttons

When you open a real pouch tin, the lid snaps. Mimic that with a 250ms CSS scale animation (1 → 0.94 → 1.03 → 1) on click. No sound, no confetti — just a tactile mechanical feeling through motion. Currently the button gives zero visual feedback on click, so users don't know if their click registered.

Make Brand Name Clickable & Visible on Cards 45 min

Product cards — brand name line

Currently the brand name on product cards is tiny (text-xs), muted, and uses a JS onclick handler instead of a proper <a> tag. Users don't realize it's clickable, and keyboard navigation skips it entirely. Fix: make it a proper link, increase to text-sm, and add an underline on hover. This drives brand page traffic and helps users browse by brand from any grid.

Niche Language: "Cans" Not "Products" 30 min

Brand cards, filter counts, search results, cart, everywhere

Replace "47 products" with "47 cans". Replace "3 items in your cart" with "3 cans in your cart". Replace "Showing 24 products" with "Showing 24 cans". Pure copy change, zero code on the design side. It's how the community talks — no real pouch user says "I bought 5 nicotine pouch products". They say "I grabbed 5 cans".

🥖

Pouch Silhouette Breadcrumb Separator 10 min

Breadcrumbs on every page

Replace the "/" breadcrumb separator with a tiny inline pouch silhouette SVG. At body-text size it reads as a soft oval — barely noticeable until you look closely. The kind of detail that gets mentioned in "attention to detail" comments. Zero visual clutter, pure brand signal.

Activate Sticky Add-to-Cart on PDP 45 min

Product detail pages — when main CTA scrolls out of view

The StickyAddToCart component already exists in the codebase but isn't rendered. When a user scrolls past the main Add to Cart button on a long PDP, a compact sticky bar should appear at the bottom with the product name, price, and an Add to Cart button. This is the single highest-conversion UX fix for product pages — users currently have to scroll back up to buy.
