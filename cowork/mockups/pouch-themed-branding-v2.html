Design Touches

Subtle Pouch-Themed Details

Not novelty gimmicks — these are small, earned design details that make the site feel like it was built by people who actually know the product.

1

Strength Colour Strip

Product cards — bottom edge of the image area

A 3px colour band at the bottom of each product card image, using the existing strength colours. No label, no icon — just colour. Anyone who's browsed pouches for 5 minutes will learn the system. Green = light, blue = normal, orange = strong, red = extra strong, purple = super strong. It's how the real tins differentiate strength visually.

SnusFriend Tin Mark

Footer, favicon, email signature, packaging

Use a miniature tin lid shape as the SnusFriend identity mark — the "SF" lockup rendered inside a circular tin lid with rim and shine. This becomes the recurring brand element: footer logo, favicon, social avatar, email header. It ties the SnusFriend identity to the product form factor itself.

Circular Product Image Containers

Product cards (grid view)

Instead of square product image containers, use circular ones — the actual shape of a pouch tin viewed from above. The product images are already cans shot from top-ish angles, so they naturally fit a circle. This makes the grid feel product-native rather than generic e-commerce.

Tiny Pouch Breadcrumb Separator

Breadcrumbs (every page)

Replace the "/" or ">" breadcrumb separator with a tiny inline SVG of a pouch silhouette. Almost invisible at body-text size, but when you notice it, it's a nice "aha" moment. This is the kind of detail that gets mentioned in design reviews and Reddit threads.

Language: "Cans" Instead of "Products"

Brand cards, filter counts, search results, cart

Across the site, replace "47 products" with "47 cans". Replace "Add to cart" with "Add to tin" or keep "Add to cart" but say "3 cans in your cart". This is pure copy — zero code change on the design side — but it makes the whole site feel like it's written by someone in the niche, not a generic Shopify template.

6

Brand Page Hero — Tin Top-Down

Individual brand pages (/brands/zyn, /brands/velo, etc.)

The brand page header already uses a gradient with the brand colour. Enhance it by adding subtle concentric ring lines (like the grip rings on a tin lid viewed from above) as a CSS background pattern. Very faint, barely visible, but it grounds the header in the product. No floating shapes, no gimmicks — just geometry that echoes the product.

7

Add-to-Cart "Snap" Feedback

Add-to-cart button

When you open a real tin, the lid makes a satisfying snap/click. On the add-to-cart button, play a tiny 50ms CSS scale animation (scale from 1 → 0.95 → 1.02 → 1) that mimics that mechanical snap. No sound, no confetti — just a subtle physical feeling through motion. It's the kind of micro-interaction that makes repeat buyers feel at home.

8

Portions-per-Can Ring on PDP

Product detail page — specs section

On the PDP, show "20 portions per can" as a ring of 20 tiny dots arranged in a circle — like looking down into an open tin and seeing the pouches arranged in a ring. It communicates quantity through the product's actual form. Much more memorable than "Portions: 20" in a specs table.
