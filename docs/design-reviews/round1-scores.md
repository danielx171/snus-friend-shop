## DESIGN 1: Dark Homepage (Theme A — "Midnight Teal")

**Reference:** velo.com, ritual.com
**Design System:** Obsidian (new — created by Stitch)
**Dimensions:** 1280 x 3082

### SCORES (1-10):
| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Visual Impact | 7 |
| E-commerce UX | 7 |
| Brand Consistency | 7 |
| Implementability | 8 |
| Mobile Readiness | 6 |
| **WEIGHTED TOTAL** | **7.0** |

*(Visual×0.25 + UX×0.25 + Brand×0.20 + Impl×0.20 + Mobile×0.10)*

### STRENGTHS:
1. Dark aurora/wave hero background is distinctive and premium — not generic gradient
2. All requested elements present: brand carousel, 4-col product grid, strength badges, pack pills, per-unit pricing
3. "The Modern Apothecary" editorial section adds brand personality
4. Teal accent color used consistently throughout

### WEAKNESSES:
1. Product card images are small relative to card size — cans should be the hero
2. 4-column grid is too dense for comfortable browsing — 3 columns with larger cards would be stronger
3. Aurora hero background, while pretty, doesn't communicate "nicotine pouches" — could be any DTC product
4. No visible free shipping progress bar in the design
5. Editorial section with stock photo feels disconnected from e-commerce purpose

### FRONTEND DESIGN CRITIQUE:
- **Typography:** Satoshi + General Sans requested and appears used — good choice, distinctive without being trendy
- **Color:** Dark charcoal + teal is committed, but the aurora hero introduces blues/purples not in the palette — needs restraint
- **Spacing:** Controlled density in the grid — appropriate for high-SKU store, but hero whitespace is too generous relative to product grid
- **Motion:** No obvious micro-interactions suggested — hover states on cards should glow teal, aurora could subtly animate
- **Differentiation:** Aurora hero is unique among competitors. Editorial section adds personality. But product grid is generic e-commerce — the cards need the SnusFriend signature (color-coded strength system)

### KEEP:
- Dark theme + teal accent palette
- Aurora/wave hero concept
- Brand carousel layout
- Editorial "Modern Apothecary" branding concept

### DISCARD:
- Stock photo in editorial section
- 4-column grid (switch to 3-column)
- Small product images

| E-commerce UX | 7 |
| Brand Consistency | 7 |
| Implementability | 9 |
| Mobile Readiness | 7 |
| **WEIGHTED TOTAL** | **7.6** |

### STRENGTHS:
1. Excellent whitespace — Ritual-level breathing room without feeling empty
2. Color-coded category cards (Fruit/Strong/New Arrivals) are intuitive and visually appealing
3. "Discover 2200+ Premium Pouches" hero is benefit-driven copy
4. "Need help choosing?" CTA + "Join Rewards Club" section adds conversion paths
5. Trust bar with icons (139 Brands, Same-Day Shipping, Rewards) builds credibility immediately

### WEAKNESSES:
1. Product cards missing pack selectors and quantity pills — the live site's key advantage
2. Strength dots (1-5) are subtle — need the explicit color-coded badge system (green/yellow/orange/red + text labels)
3. Only 3 products shown in "Best Sellers" — needs minimum 8 (2 rows of 4 or 3 rows of 3)
4. Category cards could be more prominent — they're the main discovery mechanism
5. Green accent color (#0F6E56) in places looks different from teal — palette consistency issue

### FRONTEND DESIGN CRITIQUE:
- **Typography:** Cabinet Grotesk headings + Plus Jakarta Sans body — excellent pairing, distinctive and warm
- **Color:** White + teal works beautifully. The dark green footer section creates nice contrast rhythm. But product card pricing uses a red that clashes.
- **Spacing:** Best whitespace of any design so far — the Ritual influence shows. Hero section breathes perfectly.
- **Motion:** Category cards should scale on hover. Product cards should have subtle lift shadow. Search bar could have expanding animation.
- **Differentiation:** The "Need help choosing?" CTA and Rewards Club section give this personality beyond generic e-commerce. But the product grid is too minimal for a 2200-SKU store.

### KEEP:
- Off-white (#FAFAF8) background
- Category discovery cards concept
- Trust bar with icons
- "Need help choosing?" CTA
- Rewards Club section
- Generous whitespace approach

### DISCARD:
- Minimal 3-product grid (needs 8+)
- Strength dots without color-coded labels
- Missing pack selectors

## DESIGN 3: Bold Playful Homepage (Theme C — "Neon Pouch")

**Reference:** takearecess.com, drinkolipop.com
**Design System:** Neon Pouch (new — created by Stitch)
**Dimensions:** 1280 x ~3000

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|

| E-commerce UX | 7 |
| Brand Consistency | 7 |
| Implementability | 7 |
| Mobile Readiness | 7 |
| **WEIGHTED TOTAL** | **7.5** |

### STRENGTHS:
1. "FIND YOUR FLAVOR" hero is bold and immediately communicative — best headline of all three homepages
2. Flavor-coded product cards with colored borders (mint=teal, berry=purple, citrus=warm) — exactly the SnusFriend signature system
3. "THE HOT DROP" section with category filter tabs (All, Mint, Fruit, Berry) — excellent discovery UX
4. "TOBACCO FREE. FUTURE FOCUSED." brand statement section is powerful
5. "FLAVOR TECH" section adds scientific credibility — unique among competitors
6. Dark canvas makes colorful product packaging pop dramatically

### WEAKNESSES:
1. Multiple accent colors (green, coral, purple) risk visual chaos on dense pages
2. Product cards missing pack selector pills and per-unit pricing — core SnusFriend UX
3. Strength indicators not prominently visible — need the color-coded badge system
4. "JOIN THE SQUAD" section with email capture feels dated
5. Footer is heavy and dark — could use more contrast with the already-dark body

### FRONTEND DESIGN CRITIQUE:
- **Typography:** Outfit headings are bold and impactful — tightest tracking (-0.03em) of all themes creates urgency
- **Color:** Multi-accent approach is risky but exciting. Need strict per-section color rules to prevent chaos
- **Spacing:** Good balance between density and breathing room. Product card grid spacing is appropriate
- **Motion:** Flavor-coded border glows should pulse on hover. Category tabs need smooth transition. Hero text could animate in
- **Differentiation:** Most visually distinctive of all three homepages. The flavor-coding system IS the SnusFriend signature. But needs more e-commerce substance (pricing, quantities)

### KEEP:
- "FIND YOUR FLAVOR" hero concept
- Flavor-coded product card borders
- Category filter tabs in product section
- "TOBACCO FREE. FUTURE FOCUSED." brand statement
- "FLAVOR TECH" scientific credibility section
- Dark canvas for product pop

### DISCARD:
- "JOIN THE SQUAD" email section (replace with Rewards Club)
- Missing pack selectors
- Overly heavy footer

## DESIGN 4: Product Listing Page (Theme A — "Midnight Curator")

**Reference:** nicokick.com, snusbolaget.se
**Design System:** Obsidian Teal (reused from Design 1)
**Dimensions:** 1280 x ~3500

### SCORES (1-10):
| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Visual Impact | 7 |
| E-commerce UX | 8 |
| Brand Consistency | 8 |
| Implementability | 7 |
| Mobile Readiness | 6 |
| **WEIGHTED TOTAL** | **7.35** |

### STRENGTHS:
1. Breadcrumbs + "234 products found" count + removable filter chips — complete navigation system
2. Active filters above grid is excellent UX — users always know what's applied
3. Consistent dark charcoal branding with Space Grotesk + Inter typography
4. Sticky "Scroll to Top" button for long product lists
5. Filter sidebar with brand checkboxes, flavor categories, strength badges, and format options — comprehensive faceted search

### WEAKNESSES:
1. Dark theme on a PLP can feel heavy when scanning 50+ products — light theme might work better here
2. Product card density needs validation — 3-column with detailed cards vs 4-column compact
3. Per-unit pricing calculation not visible in the grid cards
4. Strength color badges may not be prominent enough against dark card backgrounds
5. Mobile sidebar filter will need a bottom-sheet treatment — not addressed in desktop design

### FRONTEND DESIGN CRITIQUE:
- **Typography:** Space Grotesk + Inter is clean and functional for high-density scanning
- **Color:** Dark theme maintains brand consistency with Design 1. Teal accents on filter chips and CTAs are cohesive
- **Spacing:** Controlled density appropriate for a PLP — not too cramped, not too spacious
- **Motion:** Filter chips should animate in/out. Sort dropdown needs smooth open/close. Infinite scroll or paginate?
- **Differentiation:** Filter system is comprehensive. But the dark PLP is unusual — most competitors use light PLPs for scanning ease

### KEEP:
- Complete filter system (brand, flavor, strength, format)
- Removable filter chips above grid
- Product count + breadcrumbs
- Scroll to top button
- 3-column grid with adequate card spacing

### DISCARD:
- Consider switching to light theme for PLP (Theme B)
- Need per-unit pricing on cards
- Mobile filter approach needs design

## DESIGN 5: Product Detail Page (Theme A — "Midnight Teal")

**Reference:** ouraring.com, velo.com
**Design System:** Obsidian Teal (reused)
**Dimensions:** 1280 x ~2800

### SCORES (1-10):
| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Visual Impact | 8 |
| E-commerce UX | 9 |
| Brand Consistency | 8 |
| Implementability | 7 |
| Mobile Readiness | 7 |
| **WEIGHTED TOTAL** | **7.95** |

### STRENGTHS:
1. Color-coded strength selector (3mg, 6mg, 9mg) with toggle buttons — core SnusFriend UX element properly implemented
2. Segmented pack-size control (1 Can to 10-pack) with "Save" badges showing percentage discounts — drives bulk buying
3. Large teal sale price with clear savings callouts — conversion-optimized pricing display
4. "Subscribe & Save" toggle for recurring revenue — smart monetization without being pushy
5. Tabbed content area (Description/Ingredients/Reviews) keeps the page clean while allowing deep product info
6. "Customers Also Bought" carousel encourages discovery and increases AOV

### WEAKNESSES:
1. Image gallery with 4 thumbnail angles is ambitious — need actual product photography pipeline
2. Dark theme on PDP means product images need careful white-balance against charcoal background
3. Subscribe & Save toggle adds complexity — needs clear visual state management
4. "You save €2.40" callout could feel promotional/spammy if overused across all products
5. Review tab content quality depends on having actual user reviews — empty state needed

### FRONTEND DESIGN CRITIQUE:
- **Typography:** Product name at 28px bold is appropriately sized for hierarchy. Price typography with crossed-out/sale/savings creates clear value proposition
- **Color:** Dark canvas makes the teal CTA button and price pop. Strength badge colors (green/yellow/orange) are vivid against dark background
- **Spacing:** Two-column layout (gallery left, info right) is proven e-commerce standard. Adequate padding between sections
- **Motion:** Strength selector should have smooth toggle animation. Pack size pills need haptic-feeling click. Image gallery needs smooth slide transitions
- **Differentiation:** The strength selector + pack selector combo on PDP is unique. Most competitors only show one strength per URL. Subscribe & Save adds premium feel

### KEEP:
- Color-coded strength toggle buttons
- Pack selector with Save percentage badges
- Subscribe & Save toggle
- Tabbed content area
- "Customers Also Bought" carousel
- Dark canvas for product image pop

### DISCARD:
- Overly complex image gallery (simplify to main image + 2 thumbnails initially)
- Review tab without empty state handling

## DESIGN 6: Cart Drawer (Theme A — "Midnight Teal")

**Reference:** glossier.com, allbirds.com
**Design System:** Obsidian Teal (reused)
**Dimensions:** 420px x ~800px (drawer)

### SCORES (1-10):
| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Visual Impact | 8 |
| E-commerce UX | 9 |
| Brand Consistency | 9 |
| Implementability | 8 |
| Mobile Readiness | 8 |
| **WEIGHTED TOTAL** | **8.45** |

### STRENGTHS:
1. Free shipping progress bar at top — proven AOV driver, encourages adding just one more item
2. Color-coded strength badges (Green/Yellow/Orange/Red) visible on each cart item — brand signature UX carried through
3. Quantity stepper with per-unit pricing — consistent with product card UX, no context switching
4. "Frequently Bought Together" upsell section — data-driven AOV increase without being intrusive
5. Trust badges (secure checkout, 30-day returns, tobacco-free) — closes the trust gap at the critical conversion moment
6. Discount code input with "Apply" button — standard expected cart feature, well-placed
7. Clear price summary with subtotal, shipping, and total — no hidden costs

### WEAKNESSES:
1. Dark overlay drawer on dark pages could feel monotone — consider lighter cart drawer for contrast
2. 420px width is tight for detailed product info — may need to truncate product names
3. Upsell section takes space that could go to cart items on smaller screens
4. No express checkout options visible (Apple Pay, Google Pay) — missed conversion optimization
5. Remove button as red text link is subtle — could be accidentally missed

### FRONTEND DESIGN CRITIQUE:
- **Typography:** Product names in bold white, brand in teal small caps — clear hierarchy within tight space
- **Color:** Dark charcoal drawer is cohesive with the overall Midnight Teal brand. Teal CTAs and progress bar are high-contrast
- **Spacing:** Tight but controlled. Each cart item has clear boundaries. Price summary section is well-separated
- **Motion:** Drawer should slide from right with 300ms ease-out. Progress bar should animate on add. Quantity stepper needs debounce + number animation
- **Differentiation:** The strength badges + shipping progress combo in the cart is unique among competitors. Most cart drawers are generic — this is branded

### KEEP:
- Free shipping progress bar (teal fill, animated)
- Color-coded strength badges on cart items
- Quantity stepper with per-unit pricing
- "Frequently Bought Together" upsell
- Trust badges at conversion point
- Discount code input

### DISCARD:
- Consider adding express checkout (Apple Pay, Google Pay)
- Need empty cart state design
- Add animation specs for drawer slide
