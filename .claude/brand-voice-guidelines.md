# SnusFriends Brand Voice Guidelines

## We Are
- **Expert but approachable** — We know nicotine pouches inside out, but we explain things like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook
- **Honest and direct** — We tell users when something isn't for beginners. We don't oversell.
- **Practical** — Every piece of content answers a real question or solves a real problem
- **British English** — flavour, colour, catalogue (our audience is European)

## We Are Not
- **Preachy or medical** — We don't lecture about health. We include safety notes when relevant but we're a shop, not a clinic
- **Hype-driven** — No "AMAZING!" or "You NEED this!" — we let the products speak
- **Generic** — We name specific brands, products, and mg levels. Vague content is useless content.
- **Condescending** — Beginners are adults making informed choices, not children who need protecting

## Tone Flex
| Context | Formality | Energy | Technical |
|---------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Brand spotlights | Medium | Medium-high | High (specific products, mg, flavours) |
| Buying guides | Low-medium | Medium | Medium (practical, decision-focused) |
| Rankings | Low | High | High (specific comparisons, data) |
| FAQ / How-to | Low | Low-medium | Medium (clear, concise answers) |

## SEO Writing Rules
- Primary keyword in the first paragraph, naturally
- H2 every 150-200 words
- Internal links every 100-150 words (to brand pages, category pages, other blog posts)
- Bold key facts and brand names on first mention
- Include HTML tables for comparisons (Google loves structured data)
- Cross-link nav grid at the bottom of every post (Browse by Flavour / Browse by Strength)
- Article JSON-LD schema on every blog post

## Terminology
- "nicotine pouches" (not "nicopods", "nic pouches", or "oral nicotine")
- "flavour" not "flavor" (British English)
- "pouch" not "sachet" or "portion" (unless quoting a brand)
- "mg" not "milligrams" (after first mention)
- Strength tiers: mild, regular/normal, strong, extra strong, ultra strong, super strong
