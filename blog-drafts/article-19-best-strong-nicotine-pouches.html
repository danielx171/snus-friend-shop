What Counts as "Strong"? Understanding the 10–20mg Range

Nicotine strength isn't as simple as the mg number on the tin. Three factors determine how hard a pouch hits:

  • Nicotine concentration: 10–20mg per pouch is the defined range here — roughly double a regular pouch (3–7mg)
  • Moisture content: Wetter pouches release nicotine faster; drier ones deliver a slower, longer-lasting sensation
  • pH (alkalinity): Higher pH increases nicotine bioavailability, so even a 12mg pouch with high pH can feel as strong as a 15mg pouch with lower pH

This is why direct comparisons matter less than your personal tolerance and preference. A 13mg product from one manufacturer might feel noticeably stronger or weaker than a competitor's 13mg offering. Safety First: Using Strong Nicotine Pouches Responsibly

⚠️ Important Safety Information

Strong nicotine pouches are not for beginners. If you're new to nicotine, start with regular strength (3–7mg) and only progress to strong options after weeks of consistent use. The following guidance applies to experienced users only:

  • Tolerance builds fast: Your body adapts within 1–2 weeks at a higher strength. Rotate between strengths or brands to keep the experience fresh and prevent dependency escalation
  • Watch for side effects: Nicotine overuse can cause nausea, dizziness, headaches, or anxiety. If you experience these, switch to a lower strength immediately
  • Don't mix strengths: Using a 20mg pouch after a 10mg pouch multiplies the effect. Space them out by at least 30 minutes
  • Stay hydrated: Nicotine is a diuretic; drink plenty of water to minimise headaches and dry mouth
  • Pregnant/breastfeeding? Avoid nicotine entirely. Strong pouches are especially risky for expectant and nursing mothers
  • Heart conditions or hypertension: Consult your doctor before using strong nicotine products. High doses can raise heart rate and blood pressure

Nicotine is addictive. If you're using strong pouches daily, you're likely dependent. This isn't a moral judgement — it's biology. Know the difference between use and abuse, and take breaks when you need them.

Why Strength Levels Vary: What You Need to Know

Two pouches labelled "13mg" can feel completely different. Here's why:

  • Format differences: Slim pouches release nicotine slower than regular or super-slim formats due to reduced surface area
  • Base material: Some brands use tobacco leaf; others use cellulose or plant fibre. Each affects alkalinity and delivery speed
  • Manufacturer tolerance: A "13mg" pouch might actually contain 12.5–13.5mg depending on production quality. Premium brands tighter tolerances
  • Individual variation: Your metabolism, body weight, stomach contents, and genetics affect how quickly you absorb nicotine. What's perfect for one person might feel weak or overwhelming to another

This is why trying a few different brands in the same strength range makes sense — you'll find the one that feels just right for you.

A Note on Ultra-Strong Pouches (20mg+)

Products like Zeus (25mg), ICEBERG (30mg), Rabbit (50mg), and Siberia (49.5mg) exist, and some users love them. However, we recommend these only for very experienced users with established tolerance. If you're considering jumping from 15mg to 30mg, we'd advise building up gradually through the 10–20mg range first. The 20mg ceiling offers the best risk-to-reward balance for strong nicotine enjoyment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I'm using regular strength pouches. Can I jump straight to 15mg?
A: We'd recommend starting at 10–12mg first. Jumping directly to 15mg from regular (3–7mg) doubles your nicotine intake and increases the risk of side effects like nausea or dizziness. Spend a week or two at 10–12mg to let your body adapt, then move up if you're comfortable. Read our strength selector for more guidance.
Q: Why do some 12mg pouches feel stronger than 15mg pouches?
A: Perceived strength depends on format, moisture, and pH — not just the mg number. A wet, high-pH 12mg pouch can deliver nicotine faster and more intensely than a dry, low-pH 15mg pouch. Moisture content is usually the biggest factor: wetter = faster hit. This is why testing multiple brands is important.
Q: Is it safe to use strong pouches every day?
A: It's possible but not ideal. Daily use of 15–20mg pouches creates physical dependence quickly and can lead to tolerance escalation (needing stronger pouches over time). We recommend rotating strengths — use 15mg some days, drop to 10mg others — and taking 1–2 days off per week. Learn more about managing side effects.
Q: What's the difference between "Extra Strong" and "Strong"?
A: There's no universal standard. "Extra Strong" typically means 11–14mg, whilst "Strong" can mean 10–13mg. Always check the mg content on the tin rather than relying on labels. Some brands use different naming entirely (e.g., "Mighty" or "Full Charge").
Q: I'm getting side effects. What should I do?
A: First, switch to a lower strength immediately — drop to 10mg or even back to regular (5–7mg). Stay hydrated and avoid caffeine for a few hours. If nausea, dizziness, or chest pain persist, stop using nicotine and see a doctor. Detailed side effect information here.
